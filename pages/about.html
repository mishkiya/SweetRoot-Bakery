OUR JOURNEY
From Humble Beginnings

You know that feeling when you walk into a kitchen filled with the smell of vanilla and freshly baked bread? That’s where SweetRoot began—in my tiny apartment kitchen, flour dusting the counter like snow, and my grandmother’s handwritten recipes splattered with butter and sugar. I’m Amara, and I never planned to start a bakery.It all began with late-night baking experiments (and a few burnt cookies). But when friends kept saying,You should sell these!” I realized—this wasn’t just fun. It was meant to be.
In 2015, I risked it all: quit my job, maxed out my savings, and opened SweetRoot with a single oven and a wild dream. No fancy equipment, just my hands, local organic ingredients, and my grandma’s wisdom:“If you wouldn’t eat it, don’t sell it.” Today? We’re still small-batch and stubbornly handmade. Brides cry happy tears over their wedding cakes. Kids press their noses against our display case. Regulars text me, “Amara, save me a slice of that lemon raspberry!”Every treat we make is a little piece of my heart—just like grandma taught me.
